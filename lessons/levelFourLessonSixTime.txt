Time to learn about time!
You could say if it was five thirty-three, beş otuz üç, but what if you wanted to be fancy.
You will learn that half past five is beşi otuz üç geçiyor!
Oh and because this is the last lesson, I feel it is important to mention that Turkish in Turkish is Türkçe

Lesson Six: Time

So here are some examples to find the pattern:

English                               Türkçe
Twenty-four to seven                = yediye yirmi dört var
Come home at Twenty-four to seven   = yediye yirmi dört kala eve gel
Twenty-four past seven              = yediyi yirmi dört geçiyor
Come home at Twenty-four past seven = yediyi yirmi dört geçe eve gel

Notice how the word "kala" ends the past sentence unless there is more too it, in which case you say "var"
Notice how the word "geçiyor" ends the past sentence unless there is more too it, in which case you say "geçe"
Notice how when it is to it is "yediye" and when it is past it is "yediyi"

For to (a e):
Bire, İyiye, Üçe, Dörde, Beşe, Altıya, Yediye, Sekize, Dokuza, Ona, On bire, On ikiye

For past (i ı ü u):
Biri, İkiyi, Üçü, Dördü, Beşi, Altıyı, Yediyi, Sekizi, Dokuzu, Onu, On biri, On ikiyi

quarter to eight     = sekize çeyrek var
fourty-five past one = biri kırk beş geçiyor
